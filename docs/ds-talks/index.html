Upcoming DS Talks

Posted on 2019, Apr 10 by Swopnil N. Shrestha 2 mins read

Hi everyone!

If you’re looking for a bright spot amidst the April snow, check out the following lectures tomorrow and Friday, presented by Justin Marschall, a 2012 Luther Grad!

Data Science Careers for Behavioral and Social Science Majors Thursday, April 11 6:30 pm Valders 362 In an emergent, multidisciplinary field, data scientists come from diverse backgrounds. This talk highlights how one Luther 2012 psychology major pursued a career in the field of data science, while giving advice for other social science majors to do the same. Topics of discussion will include training and preparation, data science as a fulfilling career, and how to get your first job.

R You Ready? Taking Small Steps Toward Becoming an R Statistics User Friday, April 12 4:00 pm Valders 350 R is a versatile, open-source programming language that is popular in statistical and data science applications. This 60-minute talk will cover what R is, how to get it, why you want to learn it, and how to begin. In the following optional 60 minutes, a hands-on seminar will cover 10 essential packages that every R user should know; guiding new and intermediate users as they learn the language.

Remember, Hackathon is coming up this Saturday! There is no need to formally register for the event, but if you can indicate either “Going” or “Interested” on the event page, that would be great!

We hope you have a great rest of your week!
